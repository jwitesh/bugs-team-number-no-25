Chapter 1: Sorting Materials into Groups

Classification of objects based on properties like appearance, hardness, solubility, transparency, and magnetism. Grouping materials as metals, wood, plastic, glass, etc.

Chapter 2: Separation of Substances

Methods of separating substances: handpicking, sieving, winnowing, filtration, evaporation, and decantation. Concepts of soluble and insoluble substances. Use of water as a solvent.

Chapter 3:Changes Around Us

Reversible and irreversible changes. Examples of physical changes (melting, freezing) and chemical changes (burning, rusting). Expansion and contraction of materials due to heating and cooling.

Chapter 4: Fibre to Fabric

Different types of fibers: natural fibers (cotton, wool, silk) and synthetic fibers (nylon, polyester). The process of making fabric from fibers: ginning, spinning, weaving, and knitting.
