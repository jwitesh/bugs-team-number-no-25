Chapter 1:Physical and Chemical Changes

Understanding the difference between physical and chemical changes Examples of physical changes (melting, freezing, evaporation) Examples of chemical changes (rusting of iron, burning of paper) Characteristics of chemical changes: new substance formation, color change, gas release, heat production Reversible and irreversible changes

Chapter 2: Acids, Bases, and Salts

Introduction to acids, bases, and salts Properties of acids (sour taste, react with metals) and bases (bitter taste, slippery feel) Common examples of acids (lemon juice, vinegar) and bases (soap, baking soda) Use of indicators (litmus paper, phenolphthalein) to identify acids and bases Neutralization reaction: acids react with bases to form salt and water Importance of neutralization in daily life (antacids, soil treatment) Use of water as a solvent.

Chapter 3:Water: A Precious Resource

Importance of water in life processes Water cycle: evaporation, condensation, precipitation Conservation of water and methods to prevent water wastage Scarcity of water and the need for sustainable water use Role of groundwater and rainwater harvesting in water conservation

Chapter 4: Fibres and Frabrics

Different types of fibers: natural fibers (cotton, wool, silk) and synthetic fibers (nylon, polyester). The process of making fabric from fibers: ginning, spinning, weaving, and knitting.
