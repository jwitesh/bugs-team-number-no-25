Class 6 Social Science

Indus Valley Civilization

The Indus Valley Civilization was one of the world's earliest urban civilizations, thriving around 2500 BCE in what is now Pakistan and northwest India. It is known for its advanced urban planning, including well-organized cities like Harappa and Mohenjo-Daro. The civilization had a sophisticated drainage system, standardized weights and measures, and a script that remains undeciphered.

Ancient Egypt

Ancient Egypt, located along the Nile River, is famous for its rich history, including the construction of monumental pyramids and the Sphinx. The civilization was ruled by Pharaohs, who were considered divine. Ancient Egyptians made significant contributions to writing, with the development of hieroglyphics, and they practiced elaborate burial rituals to honor their deceased.

Ancient Greece

Ancient Greece is known as the cradle of Western civilization. It was composed of city-states like Athens and Sparta, each with its own government and culture. Ancient Greeks made lasting contributions to philosophy, politics, and the arts. They are well known for their myths, dramatic works, and the Olympic Games, which were held in honor of the gods.

The Roman Empire

The Roman Empire was one of the most powerful civilizations in history, known for its extensive territory and influence across Europe, North Africa, and the Middle East. It is famous for its contributions to architecture, including the construction of roads, aqueducts, and monumental structures like the Colosseum. The Roman legal system and Latin language had a profound impact on Western civilization.
