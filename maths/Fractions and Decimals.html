Fractions and Decimals

1. Introduction to Fractions

A fraction represents a part of a whole. It consists of two numbers: a numerator and a denominator.
Example: In the fraction 3/4, 3 is the numerator, and 4 is the denominator.

2. Types of Fractions

Fractions can be classified into:

  • Proper Fractions: The numerator is less than the denominator. Example: 3/5
  • Improper Fractions: The numerator is greater than or equal to the denominator. Example: 7/4
  • Mixed Fractions: A combination of a whole number and a fraction. Example: 2 1/2

3. Operations with Fractions

  • Addition and Subtraction: Fractions with the same denominator can be added or subtracted directly. Example: 1/4 + 2/4 = 3/4
  • Multiplication: Multiply the numerators and the denominators. Example: 2/3 × 3/4 = 6/12 = 1/2
  • Division: Multiply by the reciprocal of the second fraction. Example: 2/3 ÷ 3/4 = 2/3 × 4/3 = 8/9

4. Decimals

Decimals represent fractions in a different form. They use a point (decimal point) to separate the whole number from the fractional part.
Example: 0.5 is equivalent to the fraction 1/2.

5. Converting Fractions to Decimals

To convert a fraction to a decimal, divide the numerator by the denominator.
Example: 3/4 = 0.75

6. Converting Decimals to Fractions

To convert a decimal to a fraction, write the decimal as a fraction with 1 as the denominator and multiply both the numerator and denominator by a power of 10.
Example: 0.25 = 25/100 = 1/4

7. Comparing Fractions and Decimals

  • Fractions: Written in the form a/b where a is the numerator and b is the denominator.
  • Decimals: Written with a decimal point separating the whole number from the fractional part. Example: 1.25.
