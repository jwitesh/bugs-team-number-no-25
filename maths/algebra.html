Algebra

1. Introduction to Algebra

Algebra is a branch of mathematics dealing with symbols and the rules for manipulating those symbols.
These symbols (variables) represent quantities without fixed values. In algebra, we work with variables, constants, and arithmetic operations to solve equations and understand patterns.

2. Basic Algebraic Expressions

An algebraic expression is a combination of variables, constants, and operations such as addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division.
Example: 2x + 5, where x is a variable and 5 is a constant.

3. Solving Simple Equations

An equation is a mathematical statement that shows the equality between two expressions.
Example: x + 5 = 12
To solve the equation, we need to isolate the variable:

  • Subtract 5 from both sides: x = 12 - 5
  • Result: x = 7

4. Like Terms and Unlike Terms

- Like Terms: Terms that have the same variable raised to the same power. These can be combined by adding or subtracting their coefficients.
Example: 2x + 3x = 5x
- Unlike Terms: Terms that have different variables or powers, and cannot be combined directly.
Example: 2x + 5y (cannot be simplified).

5. Laws of Algebra

Algebra follows specific rules or laws that make manipulation of expressions possible.
The main laws of algebra include:

  • Commutative Law:
    • For Addition: a + b = b + a
    • For Multiplication: a × b = b × a
  • Associative Law:
    • For Addition: (a + b) + c = a + (b + c)
    • For Multiplication: (a × b) × c = a × (b × c)
  • Distributive Law: a(b + c) = ab + ac

6. Factorization

Factorization is the process of breaking down an algebraic expression into simpler terms (factors) that, when multiplied together, give the original expression.
Example: x^2 + 5x = x(x + 5)
This process is useful in simplifying expressions and solving equations.

7. Linear Equations

A linear equation is an equation where the highest power of the variable is 1.
Example: 2x + 3 = 7
Steps to solve:

  • Subtract 3 from both sides: 2x = 7 - 3
  • Divide both sides by 2: x = 4 / 2
  • Result: x = 2

8. Word Problems in Algebra

Algebra is often used to solve real-life problems. These are represented as word problems that can be translated into algebraic equations.
Example: If the sum of a number and 10 is 30, find the number.
Let the number be x.
Equation: x + 10 = 30
Solution: x = 30 - 10 = 20
