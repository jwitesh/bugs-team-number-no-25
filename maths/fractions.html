Fractions

1. Introduction to Fractions

A fraction represents a part of a whole. It consists of two numbers:

  • Numerator: The number at the top, representing the number of parts you have.
  • Denominator: The number at the bottom, representing the total number of equal parts.
For example, in the fraction 3/4, 3 is the numerator and 4 is the denominator, meaning 3 parts out of 4.

2. Types of Fractions

There are several types of fractions:

  • Proper Fractions: The numerator is smaller than the denominator (e.g., 3/5).
  • Improper Fractions: The numerator is greater than or equal to the denominator (e.g., 7/4).
  • Mixed Fractions: A combination of a whole number and a proper fraction (e.g., 2 1/3).
  • Unit Fractions: The numerator is always 1 (e.g., 1/2, 1/3).

3. Equivalent Fractions

Equivalent fractions are fractions that represent the same value, even though they have different numerators and denominators.
For example, 1/2 is equivalent to 2/4 and 3/6.
To find equivalent fractions, multiply or divide both the numerator and denominator by the same number.
Example: 1/2 = 2/4 = 3/6

4. Simplifying Fractions

Simplifying a fraction means expressing it in its simplest form, where the numerator and denominator have no common factors other than 1.
For example, 4/8 can be simplified to 1/2 by dividing both the numerator and denominator by 4.

5. Addition and Subtraction of Fractions

Addition: To add fractions with the same denominator, simply add the numerators. For fractions with different denominators, first find a common denominator.
Example:
1/4 + 1/4 = 2/4 = 1/2
1/2 + 1/3 = (3/6) + (2/6) = 5/6

Subtraction: To subtract fractions with the same denominator, subtract the numerators. For fractions with different denominators, find a common denominator first.
Example:
3/4 - 1/4 = 2/4 = 1/2
3/5 - 1/3 = (9/15) - (5/15) = 4/15

6. Multiplication and Division of Fractions

Multiplication: To multiply fractions, multiply the numerators together and the denominators together.
Example: 2/3 × 3/4 = (2×3) / (3×4) = 6/12 = 1/2

Division: To divide fractions, multiply the first fraction by the reciprocal of the second fraction (flip the numerator and denominator of the second fraction).
Example: 2/3 ÷ 1/4 = 2/3 × 4/1 = 8/3

7. Converting Between Improper Fractions and Mixed Numbers

To convert an improper fraction to a mixed number, divide the numerator by the denominator. The quotient is the whole number, and the remainder becomes the numerator of the fraction.
Example: 7/4 = 1 3/4

To convert a mixed number to an improper fraction, multiply the whole number by the denominator and add the numerator.
Example: 2 1/3 = (2×3 + 1)/3 = 7/3
