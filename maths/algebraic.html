Algebra

1. Introduction to Algebra

Algebra is a branch of mathematics that uses symbols, letters, and numbers to express mathematical relationships and solve problems. The symbols represent numbers or quantities and are used to form equations and expressions.

2. Variables and Constants

- **Variable:** A symbol, often a letter, that represents a number in an equation or expression. Example: x, y.
- **Constant:** A fixed value that does not change. Example: 7, -3.

3. Algebraic Expressions

An algebraic expression consists of variables, constants, and operators (such as +, -, ×, /) combined together.
Example: 3x + 4y - 7 is an algebraic expression.

4. Simplifying Algebraic Expressions

To simplify an algebraic expression, combine like terms. Like terms have the same variables raised to the same power.
Example: Simplify 2x + 3x - 4 to 5x - 4.

5. Solving Linear Equations

A linear equation is an equation of the first degree. To solve it, isolate the variable on one side of the equation.
Example: Solve 2x + 3 = 7.

  • Subtract 3 from both sides: 2x = 4
  • Divide both sides by 2: x = 2

6. Using Formulas

Algebra often involves using formulas to solve problems. Common formulas include:

  • Area of a rectangle: A = l × w
  • Perimeter of a rectangle: P = 2(l + w)

7. Graphing Linear Equations

Linear equations can be graphed on a coordinate plane. The graph of a linear equation is a straight line.
Example: The equation y = 2x + 1 represents a line with a slope of 2 and a y-intercept of 1.

8. Solving Systems of Equations

Systems of equations involve two or more equations with the same variables. The solution is the point where the lines intersect.
Example: Solve the system:

  • x + y = 5
  • 2x - y = 1

By solving, you get x = 2 and y = 3.
