Geometry

1. Introduction to Geometry

Geometry is the branch of mathematics that deals with the study of shapes, sizes, positions, angles, and dimensions of things. It is foundational for many fields such as architecture, engineering, and computer graphics.

2. Points, Lines, and Angles

- Point: A location in space, usually represented by a dot.
- Line: A straight one-dimensional figure that extends infinitely in both directions. It has no thickness.
- Angle: Formed when two lines or rays meet at a common point called the vertex.
Types of angles:

  • Acute Angle: Less than 90°
  • Right Angle: Exactly 90°
  • Obtuse Angle: Greater than 90° but less than 180°
  • Straight Angle: Exactly 180°

3. Triangles

A triangle is a three-sided polygon. Triangles are classified based on their sides and angles.
- Based on sides:

  • Equilateral Triangle: All sides are equal.
  • Isosceles Triangle: Two sides are equal.
  • Scalene Triangle: All sides are different.
- Based on angles:
  • Acute Triangle: All angles are less than 90°.
  • Right Triangle: One angle is 90°.
  • Obtuse Triangle: One angle is greater than 90°.

4. Quadrilaterals

A quadrilateral is a four-sided polygon with various types based on side lengths and angles.
- Rectangle: Opposite sides are equal, and each angle is 90°.
- Square: All sides are equal, and each angle is 90°.
- Parallelogram: Opposite sides are equal, and opposite angles are equal.
- Trapezium: A quadrilateral with one pair of parallel sides.

5. Circles

A circle is a round shape where every point on the boundary is equidistant from the center.
Important parts of a circle:

  • Radius: The distance from the center to any point on the circle.
  • Diameter: The longest distance across the circle, equal to twice the radius.
  • Circumference: The distance around the circle.
  • Chord: A line segment joining two points on the circle.
  • Arc: A part of the circle's circumference.
Formula for circumference:
C = 2πr, where r is the radius.

6. Area and Perimeter

Perimeter: The total distance around a 2D shape.
Area: The amount of space inside a 2D shape.
Formulas for common shapes:

  • Rectangle: Perimeter = 2(l + w), Area = l × w
  • Square: Perimeter = 4s, Area = s²
  • Triangle: Area = ½ × base × height
  • Circle: Area = πr², Circumference = 2πr

7. Coordinate Geometry

Coordinate geometry (or analytic geometry) is the study of geometry using a coordinate plane.
The coordinate plane is divided into four quadrants by the x-axis and y-axis. Any point on the plane is represented by an ordered pair (x, y).
