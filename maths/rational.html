Rational Numbers

1. Introduction to Rational Numbers

Rational numbers are numbers that can be expressed as a fraction where both the numerator and the denominator are integers and the denominator is not zero. In other words, they are numbers that can be written in the form a/b where a and b are integers, and b ≠ 0.

2. Examples of Rational Numbers

Some examples of rational numbers include:

  • 1/2
  • -3/4
  • 5 (which can be written as 5/1)
  • 0 (which can be written as 0/1)

3. Properties of Rational Numbers

Rational numbers have several important properties:

  • Closure Property: The sum, difference, product, and quotient of two rational numbers are also rational numbers.
  • Commutative Property: Addition and multiplication of rational numbers are commutative. a/b + c/d = c/d + a/b and a/b × c/d = c/d × a/b
  • Associative Property: Addition and multiplication of rational numbers are associative. (a/b + c/d) + e/f = a/b + (c/d + e/f) and (a/b × c/d) × e/f = a/b × (c/d × e/f)
  • Distributive Property: Multiplication is distributive over addition. a/b × (c/d + e/f) = a/b × c/d + a/b × e/f

4. Comparing Rational Numbers

To compare rational numbers, convert them to have a common denominator or convert them to decimal form. Compare the numerators or decimal values to determine which is larger or smaller.
Example: Compare 3/4 and 5/6:

  • Convert to decimal: 3/4 = 0.75, 5/6 ≈ 0.833
  • 5/6 is greater than 3/4.

5. Operations with Rational Numbers

Rational numbers can be added, subtracted, multiplied, and divided.
Addition: To add rational numbers, find a common denominator and add the numerators. a/b + c/d = (ad + bc) / bd
Subtraction: Similar to addition but subtract the numerators. a/b - c/d = (ad - bc) / bd
Multiplication: Multiply the numerators and denominators. (a/b) × (c/d) = (a × c) / (b × d)
Division: Multiply by the reciprocal of the divisor. (a/b) ÷ (c/d) = (a/b) × (d/c) = (a × d) / (b × c)

6. Simplifying Rational Numbers

To simplify a rational number, divide the numerator and the denominator by their greatest common divisor (GCD).
Example: Simplify 8/12:

  • GCD of 8 and 12 is 4.
  • 8 ÷ 4 = 2, 12 ÷ 4 = 3
  • Thus, 8/12 = 2/3
