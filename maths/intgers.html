Integers

1. What are Integers?

Integers are a set of whole numbers that include positive numbers, negative numbers, and zero. They do not include fractions or decimals.

The set of integers is represented as: {..., -3, -2, -1, 0, 1, 2, 3, ...}

2. Positive and Negative Integers

- **Positive Integers**: Numbers greater than zero, like 1, 2, 3, etc.
- **Negative Integers**: Numbers less than zero, like -1, -2, -3, etc.

3. Zero

Zero (0) is a special integer. It is neither positive nor negative but plays an important role in arithmetic operations.

4. Operations with Integers

Integers follow specific rules for addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division.

Addition:

  • Adding two positive integers gives a positive result. Example: 3 + 5 = 8
  • Adding two negative integers gives a negative result. Example: -3 + (-5) = -8
  • Adding a positive and a negative integer means subtracting the smaller number from the larger and taking the sign of the larger. Example: 5 + (-3) = 2

Subtraction:

  • To subtract an integer, add its opposite. Example: 7 - (-3) = 7 + 3 = 10

Multiplication:

  • Multiplying two positive integers gives a positive result. Example: 3 × 4 = 12
  • Multiplying two negative integers also gives a positive result. Example: -3 × -4 = 12
  • Multiplying a positive and a negative integer gives a negative result. Example: 3 × -4 = -12

Division:

  • Dividing two positive integers gives a positive result. Example: 12 ÷ 4 = 3
  • Dividing two negative integers gives a positive result. Example: -12 ÷ -4 = 3
  • Dividing a positive integer by a negative integer gives a negative result. Example: 12 ÷ -4 = -3

5. Properties of Integers

Integers follow specific properties for arithmetic operations:

  • Commutative Property: a + b = b + a and a × b = b × a
  • Associative Property: (a + b) + c = a + (b + c) and (a × b) × c = a × (b × c)
  • Distributive Property: a × (b + c) = a × b + a × c

6. Absolute Value

The absolute value of an integer is its distance from zero on the number line, regardless of direction. It's always a positive number or zero.
For example, |-5| = 5 and |7| = 7.

7. Number Line

Integers can be represented on a number line, where positive numbers are to the right of zero, and negative numbers are to the left.
